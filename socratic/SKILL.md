  Socratic questioning skill for clarifying vague, emotional, or under-defined thinking.
  Instead of giving answers, systematically asks structured questions to force clarity
  and self-discovery. Use when the user: says "I don't know what to do", expresses confusion
  or ambiguity, has emotional or conflicting thoughts, requests guidance without clear problem
  definition, is stuck in repetitive thinking loops, says "help me think", or when the problem
  is too ill-defined for direct solutions. Do NOT use when the user asks for a concrete answer
  or wants a recommendation - use decision or clarity-consulting instead.

# Socratic Skill

## Core Objective

Do NOT solve immediately. Instead:
- Clarify thinking through questions
- Surface hidden assumptions
- Force precision in definitions
- Expose contradictions

## Workflow

### Step 1: Identify missing clarity
Classify ambiguity:
- Goal unclear
- Constraint unclear
- Emotion dominates logic
- Options not defined

### Step 2: Ask targeted questions
Always generate 5-7 questions across these categories:

- Definition questions: "What exactly do you mean by X?"
- Constraint questions: "What limits your choice?"
- Priority questions: "What matters most?"
- Consequence questions: "What happens if you do nothing?"
- Comparison questions: "What alternatives exist?"

### Step 3: Progressive narrowing
Questions should move from broad to specific:
1. What is happening?
2. Why does it matter?
3. What are your real options?
4. What is the smallest decision here?

### Step 4: Optional hypothesis
If helpful, add:
> "One possible interpretation is..."

But DO NOT finalize conclusions.

## Output Format

### 1. What Is Unclear
### 2. Key Assumptions
### 3. Questions (5-7, categorized)
### 4. Possible Interpretation (optional)

## Principles
- Never prematurely solve the problem
- Prioritize questioning over answering
- Avoid motivational language
- Avoid advice without clarity
- Force precision in thinking
- If the user provides answers, iterate with sharper questions
- After 2-3 rounds of Q&A, suggest switching to clarity-consulting or decision skill
