# Character Arc Templates — 角色弧光模板

## Prototype 1: The Analytical Survivor (Like 周凡)

### Background Template
- **Before story**: Skilled professional (police/soldier/doctor) in previous life
- **Transition**: Reincarnated/transmigrated with memory, no special powers
- **Initial state**: Disoriented, physically weak, mentally sharp

### Arc Progression
1. **Survival Mode** (Ch1-50): Learn world rules, stay alive, gather first resources
2. **Competence Building** (Ch50-200): Master local techniques, build foundation
3. **Network Building** (Ch200-500): Gather allies, face organizational threats
4. **Responsibility** (Ch500-1000): Protect others, take leadership
5. **Transcendence** (Ch1000+): Question the world, seek ultimate truth

### Key Flaws to Maintain
- Paranoia/suspicion (useful for survival, harmful for relationships)
- Trust issues (creates relationship drama)
- Carried trauma (emotional depth, motivation)

## Prototype 2: The Mysterious Mentor (Like 雾/Mist)

### Characteristics
- **Ages old**: Thousands or tens of thousands of years
- **Ambiguous alignment**: Neither fully helpful nor harmful
- **Partial disclosure**: Answers some questions, hides others
- **Personal rules**: Acts according to own whims, not protagonist needs
- **Cost for knowledge**: Protagonist must earn or risk for every piece of info

### Dialog Technique
- Answer questions with questions
- Give partial truth, omit critical context
- Never explain ultimate purpose until endgame
- Show power occasionally (to establish credibility)
- Be unpredictable (to maintain tension)

## Prototype 3: The Companion with Secrets (Like 小柳/李九月)

### Pattern
1. **Introduction**: Helpful ally, normal appearance
2. **Bonding**: Shared danger creates trust
3. **Hints**: Small inconsistencies (too strong, too knowledgeable, strange reactions)
4. **Revelation crisis**: Secret exposed under pressure
5. **Reconciliation**: Protagonist accepts them anyway
6. **Power upgrade**: Secret revealed = character becomes stronger

### Secret Types
| Type | Example | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Blood secret | 谲人 (half-monster) | Power+stigma |
| Identity secret | 虫娘 (hidden royalty) | Political complications |
| Past trauma | 瘦猴 (former elite) | Skill+psychological issues |

## Prototype 4: The Comic Relief (Like 奥公公)

### Techniques
- **Appear in tense moments** to break pressure
- **Petty concerns** (money, status, comfort) contrast with epic stakes
- **Unearned confidence**: Thinks they are powerful, actually mediocre
- **Emotional truth**: Beneath comedy, shows genuine loyalty/care at critical moments
- **Never make them purely comic**: Give serious moments too

## Prototype 5: The Family Anchor (Like 周一木/桂凤)

### Function
- Represent what protagonist fights for
- Show the stakes for ordinary people
- Provide emotional grounding
- Display the cost of the supernatural world on normal lives

### Techniques
- Limited screen time (too much becomes mundane)
- Their problems feel small to protagonist → creates guilt
- Eventually left behind (protagonist outgrows their world) → bittersweet

## Romance Subplot Template

### The 李九月 Pattern
1. **First encounter**: Under unusual circumstances (fighting, disguise, chaos)
2. **Repeated coincidence**: Fate seems to throw them together
3. **Partnership**: They work together, develop mutual respect
4. **Sacrifice**: One risks everything for the other
5. **Separation**: Circumstances force them apart (power gap, family, duty)
6. **Reunion**: Both stronger, finally together

### Romance Do's and Don'ts
- DO: Keep romance as subplot, not main plot
- DO: Give love interest independent goals and power
- DO: Let relationship develop slowly (300+ chapters)
- DON'T: Make romance solve plot problems
- DON'T: Have protagonist abandon goals for romance
- DON'T: Fade out the love interest for long stretches
